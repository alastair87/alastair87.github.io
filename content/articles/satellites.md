Sometimes stupid questions make the best questions.

Take this video below the question is posed of why when the Earth is seen from the Artemis II lunar mission we cannot see any of the many thousands of satellites. 

{{< youtube-short glONHIg52aE >}}

We're given a sharp takedown by the YouTuber Mercedes Chandler who correctly points out that we don't see the satellites because they are so small, much like we don't see mosquitoes in a forest photo or humans in a photo of a city.

On the face of it this seems like a great opportunity to call out a possibly bad faith question but it also seems like a missed opportunity to ask a question we think we all know the answer to but probably don't:

> Why are small things harder to see than big things?
